10 FAQS about National Parks:

What government agency oversees the National Park Service?
The National Park Service is a burea of the Department of Interior
How many employees are in the National Park Service?
Approximately 20,000 and more than 279,000 volunteers in 2019
How old is the National Park System?
The National Park Service was created by an act signed by President Woodrow Wilson on August 25, 1916.
What is the largest national park site?
Wrangell-St. Elias National Park and Preserve, AK, at 13.2 million acres
What is the smallest national park site?
Smallest: Thaddeus Kosciuszko National Memorial, PA, at 0.02 acres
How many people visit the national parks?
Total recreation visitors to the national parks in 2019: 327,516,619.
What is the most-visited national park?
Golden Gate National Recreation Area. 15 million
What is the National Park Service budget?
FY 2017 Enacted: $2.932 billion
Can I bring my pet to a national park?
Some national parks welcome pets—in developed areas, on many trails and campgrounds, and in some lodging facilities.
Can I bring my service dog to a national park?
Yes, service dogs are legally permitted anywhere in a park that visitors can go.

Big Bend National Park

  • Big Bend National Park covers a total area of 801,163 acres
  • Big Bend was made a national park on June 20, 1935
  • The highest elevation found in Big Bend is 7,832 feet on Emory Peak
  • The lowest elevation found in Big Bend is 1,715 feet at the Rio Grande River

San Antonio Missions National Historic Park

  • Nation's largest collection of Spanish colonial resources
  • Theodore Roosevelt visited Mission Concepción in 1898 when he was in San Antonio with the Rough Riders
  • The first cattle drives originated from San Antonio's Spanish missions
  • The missions weren't just churches in the 18th century, they were self-sustaining towns

Guadalupe Mountains National Park

  • Guadalupe Mountains National Park covers a total area of 86,416 acres
  • Guadalupe Mountains was made a national park on October 15, 1966
  • The highest elevation found in Guadalupe Mountains is 8,751 feet on Guadalupe Peak
  • The lowest elevation found in Guadalupe Mountains is 3,636 feet near Williams Road
